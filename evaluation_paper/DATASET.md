## Data provenance and speaker consent

The evaluation recordings were created for this retrospective pilot by author-speakers.
Each speaker consented to public release of the recordings, transcripts, system outputs,
references, and annotation rows for research reproducibility.

The input recordings are retained in their available source formats (`.wav` or `.m4a`),
and the exact released audio path for each lecture is recorded in
`manifest/condition_manifest.csv`.

The audio files contain identifiable voices. Speaker IDs are coded in the release, but
the audio should not be considered anonymized. The recordings contain no patient data,
no third-party classroom audio, and no readings from textbooks, papers, websites, or
pre-existing lecture scripts. Speakers used AI-generated topic outlines only as loose
speaking plans and delivered the lectures extemporaneously in their own Korean-English
wording.

These recordings and transcripts are released only as research materials for evaluating
speech transcription and post-ASR processing. They are not medical or educational guidance,
and the medical accuracy of the spoken lecture content was not independently verified.
